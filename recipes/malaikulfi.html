About Malai Kulfi

Malai kulfi is a traditional Indian frozen dairy dessert, similar to ice cream but denser and creamier. Made by slowly simmering milk until thickened, it's flavored with cardamom, saffron, and often nuts like pistachios or almonds. Unlike Western ice cream, kulfi isn’t churned, giving it a solid, creamy texture. It’s typically poured into cone-shaped molds and frozen, then served on a stick or in slices. Popular across India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Nepal.

Ingredients

  • Full-fat milk
  • Sugar
  • Cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped almonds
  • chopped pistachios
  • Cornstarch or milk powder (optional, for thickening)
  • Rose water or kewra essence (optional, for aroma)

Steps to prepare Malai Kulfi:

  1. Boil 1 liter full-fat milk in a heavy-bottom pan.
  2. Simmer on low heat, stirring often, until reduced to half.
  3. Add 4–5 tbsp sugar and stir until dissolved.
  4. Add ¼ tsp cardamom powder and a few saffron strands optional.
  5. Add 1–2 tbsp chopped almonds and pistachios.
  6. Add a few drops of rose water or kewra essence optional.
  7. (Optional) Mix 1 tsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp cold milk and stir.
  8. Cook for a few more minutes until thick and creamy.
  9. Let the mixture cool completely at room temperature.
  10. Pour into kulfi molds or small cups.
  11. Insert sticks and freeze for 6–8 hours or overnight.
  12. Dip molds in warm water briefly and unmold to serve.
