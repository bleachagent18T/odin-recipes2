About

A samosa (/səˈmoʊsə/) (listenⓘ) (Hindi: समोसा) ( Persian: سمبوسه) is a fried South Asian[2] and West Asian snack. It is a pastry with a savory filling that mostly consists of vegetables like spiced potatoes, onions, and peas, but can also include meat or fish, or even cheese. Its name originates from the Persian word sambosag (سنبوسگ) (meaning 'triangular pastry'). It is made in different shapes, including triangular, cone, or crescent, depending on the region.[3][4][5] Samosas are often accompanied by chutney, and have origins in medieval times or earlier.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Ajwain
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Water
  • Potatoes
  • Green peas
  • Green chilies
  • Ginger
  • Coriander powder
  • Cumin seeds
  • Amchur
  • Red chili powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Steps to prepare samosa:

  1. Mix flour, salt, ajwain, and oil; rub until crumbly.
  2. Add lukewarm water gradually and knead into a firm dough.
  3. Cover and rest the dough for 30–40 minutes
  4. Heat oil, add cumin seeds, ginger, and green chilies.
  5. Add mashed potatoes and boiled peas; stir well.
  6. Add spices: coriander, garam masala, amchur, turmeric, red chili, and salt.
  7. Mix thoroughly; finish with chopped coriander leaves. Let cool.
  8. Divide dough into balls and roll into ovals.
  9. Cut each oval in half to form two semi-ovals.
  10. Fold each into a cone and seal the edge with water.
  11. Fill the cone with the potato mixture.
  12. Seal the top edge tightly with water.
  13. Heat oil on medium-low flame in a deep pan.
  14. Fry samosas slowly until golden brown and crisp.
  15. Drain on paper and serve hot.
